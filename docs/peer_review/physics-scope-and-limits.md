# Physics Scope and Limits

## Plain-Language Summary

The repository now has a real physics extension, but it is not a universal physics theory.

The strongest physics content is concentrated in:
- exact projection-compatible systems,
- asymptotic constraint-damping systems,
- and exact or sector-based correction structures tied to QEC.

## What Physics Systems Genuinely Fit

### Strong exact fit
- periodic Helmholtz/Leray projection
- Maxwell / Coulomb-gauge transverse projection on compatible domains
- QEC sector recovery as the exact discrete anchor

### Strong asymptotic or conditional fit
- GLM divergence cleaning
- numerical-relativity constraint damping
- continuous quantum error correction with feedback or engineered dissipation

## What Is Reinterpretation Of Known Structure

The repository does not claim novelty for:
- Helmholtz/Leray projection,
- Coulomb-gauge projection,
- Knill-Laflamme or stabilizer structure,
- GLM divergence cleaning,
- or standard constraint-damping formulations.

The added value is the protected-state correction architecture that keeps exact, asymptotic, and failure structures in one disciplined framework.

## What Is Rejected

The repository rejects or demotes:
- naive periodic-projector reuse on bounded-domain problems,
- generic constrained Hamiltonian systems without an explicit correction operator,
- and broad physics-forward naming that outruns the actual theorem spine.

## Strongest Physics-Side Contribution

The strongest physics-side contribution is not a new universal theorem.
It is a sharper classification:
- exact projector-compatible correction,
- asymptotic damping-based correction,
- and explicit no-go structure for invalid transplant or ambiguity claims.

## Reviewer Guidance

If reading this repo from a physics perspective, the fairest interpretation is:
- theory-first,
- physics-extended,
- theorem-driven where possible,
- and conservative about novelty.
