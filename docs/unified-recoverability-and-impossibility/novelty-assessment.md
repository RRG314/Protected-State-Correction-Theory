# Novelty Assessment

## Bottom line

This branch is **not** claiming a new universal theory of recoverability.
That broader claim failed repeatedly and is now part of the branch's result.

## Standard or standard-adjacent pieces

Almost certainly standard:
- fiber-factorization exactness,
- fiber-collision impossibility,
- coarsening monotonicity,
- restricted-linear kernel/row-space exactness,
- many field dictionaries once the right target is chosen.

## Repo-new but not necessarily literature-new

Likely repo-new formulation / synthesis:
- the unified exact-vs-detectable-only-vs-asymptotic vocabulary tied explicitly to this theorem-first repo,
- the branch-level formal statement that universal exactness stops at the fiber level while stronger amount-only classifiers fail,
- the executable branch artifact layer tying together same-rank witnesses, fixed-library same-budget witnesses, weaker-vs-stronger noisy witnesses, and exact-versus-asymptotic witnesses.

## Strongest candidate for a real contribution

Strongest surviving contribution candidate:
- the **no rank-only exact classifier theorem** as a clean negative theorem inside the restricted-linear class,
- the fixed-library same-budget strengthening of that theorem,
- and the quantitative noisy weaker-versus-stronger separation theorem.

Honest status:
- mathematically real,
- probably modest,
- likely closer to a sharp structural theorem/falsification package than to a big literature-disrupting claim.

## What is explicitly not claimed as novel

Not promoted as novelty:
- using QEC, control, or inverse-problem language at all,
- saying that recovery depends on distinguishability,
- saying that weaker targets are easier,
- saying that coarse measurements can lose information.

Those are important, but not new by themselves.

## Best publication-worthy angle if pushed further

If this branch were pushed toward publication, the strongest angle would be:
- a restricted theorem/falsification paper showing exactly where universal recoverability language stops,
- anchored by the no-rank-only theorem,
- strengthened by the fixed-library same-budget countertheorem and the noisy weaker-versus-stronger separation theorem,
- with explicit cross-field dictionaries and boundary statements.

That is much stronger than a survey-like “common structure across fields” pitch.
