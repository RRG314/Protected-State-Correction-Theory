# Stopping-Logic Novelty and Usefulness Assessment

## Novelty

This idea is **not** strong as novelty in the sense of a new mathematical branch identity.

Most of the underlying content is already carried by:
- impossibility theorems,
- target-hierarchy theorems,
- augmentation theorems,
- and false-positive / fragility results.

So the stopping layer is:
- `repo-new` as an explicit packaging of decisions,
- likely `literature-known in spirit`,
- and not strong enough to claim a distinct outside theorem program on its own.

## Usefulness

Its usefulness is much stronger than its novelty.

It helps by making the branch answer:
- when exact pursuit should stop,
- when the right move is to weaken the target,
- when augmentation is justified,
- when architecture should change,
- and when a positive exactness claim should not be promoted.

That is valuable for:
- inverse-problem false-positive control,
- workbench guidance,
- and reviewer-facing honesty.

## Final assessment

- novelty as a standalone theory: `low`
- usefulness as a theorem-linked decision layer: `high`
- promotion level deserved now: **small integrated layer only**
