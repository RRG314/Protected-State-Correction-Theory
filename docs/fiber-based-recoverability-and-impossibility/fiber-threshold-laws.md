# Fiber Threshold Laws On Benchmark Families

## General interpretation

A threshold appears when a nested record family becomes fine enough that its fibers stop mixing target values.

## Periodic modal family

- periodic support/cutoff thresholds are the point where the retained modal record separates every protected mode used by the target
- before threshold: fibers still mix states differing on hidden modes
- at threshold: fibers become target-constant for that target

## Diagonal/history family

- history thresholds are the point where the time-window record separates the chosen protected functional
- before threshold: horizon fibers still mix target values
- after threshold: the functional becomes constant on those history fibers

## Qubit phase-loss family

- fixed-basis measurement fibers mix phase-sensitive states
- the weaker z-coordinate target is fiber-constant even though the full Bloch target is not

## Branch status

These are not promoted as one universal threshold law.
They are family-level fiber-threshold laws with different underlying geometry.
