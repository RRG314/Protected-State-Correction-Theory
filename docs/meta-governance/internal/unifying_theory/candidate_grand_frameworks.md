# Candidate Grand Frameworks

Date: 2026-04-17

## Candidate A: Structure-Dependent Recoverability Theory (SDRT)

### Formal statement (candidate)
Recoverability status of target `T` from records `M` on family `A` is determined by structural compatibility (fiber/quotient/compatibility relations), not by amount-only summaries.

### Covers well
- OCP recoverability/fiber branch.
- Restricted-linear anti-classifier and augmentation package.
- Soliton recoverability modulo symmetry lane.

### Distorts
- MHD closure branch if treated as pure observation inversion only.
- SDS engineering/emergence lanes (not recoverability-complete).

### Decision
`PROMOTE` as main theorem-oriented umbrella **for recoverability content only**.

## Candidate B: Structure-Dependent Recoverability and Failure Theory (SDRFT)

### Formal statement (candidate)
Exactness and impossibility are co-governed by compatibility and collision structures, with explicit fragility under family enlargement, model mismatch, and architecture/domain mismatch.

### Covers well
- OCP no-go spine plus anti-classifier/fragility package.
- Bounded-domain obstruction and finite-mode exact subcase.
- Soliton projection/no-go split and same-count failures (restricted).
- MHD obstruction/survivor structure (declared families).

### Distorts
- Requires careful separation of theorem-grade vs validated/conditional pieces.
- Can overreach if presented as universal nonlinear law.

### Decision
`PROMOTE` as strongest branch-limited unifying framework candidate.

## Candidate C: Structure-Dependent Recoverability, Preservation, and Emergence Theory (SDRPET)

### Formal statement (candidate)
One theory jointly explains recoverability/correction, operator preservation/failure, and autonomous emergence/self-organization.

### Covers well
- Conceptual storytelling across OCP + soliton discovery + SDS engineering.

### Distorts
- Conflates operator-driven correction with autonomous nonlinear emergence.
- Upgrades analogy-only links to fake theorem links.
- Breaks current falsification discipline.

### Decision
`REJECT` as current theorem framework; keep only as conceptual future research question.

## Framework Promotion Outcome

- **Primary promoted framework:** Candidate B (SDRFT), branch-limited and status-disciplined.
- **Secondary scoped framework:** Candidate A (recoverability-only theorem core).
- **Rejected for now:** Candidate C.
