# SFPR Literature Audit

Status: `EXPLORATION / NON-PROMOTED`

This audit positions the SFPR candidate against established areas and classifies overlap risk claim-by-claim.

## Method

For each layer, we compared the mathematical role of SFPR objects against established formulations in observability, target-oriented design, functional compression/computation, causal identifiability, data-driven control, and structure/pattern literature. We treated "new wording" as non-novel unless it added a stricter condition, stronger theorem scope, or new falsifiable invariant.

## Layer-by-Layer Positioning

## I. Structure Ontology

Nearest literature zones:
- structural information and representation frameworks,
- equivalence-class / quotient viewpoints,
- function-oriented observability abstractions.

Assessment:
- Core `tau/M/fiber` ontology is `LITERATURE-KNOWN` in substance.
- SFPR contribution opportunity is not ontology alone; it is the explicit cross-layer use of target-relative structure under context and design constraints.

Risk:
- high risk of repackaging if structure ontology is presented without theorem deltas.

## II. Structure Formation

Nearest literature zones:
- self-organization and pattern-formation dynamics,
- symmetry breaking and constraint-induced organization,
- optimization-induced representation change.

Assessment:
- Generic "formation" language is `LITERATURE-KNOWN` and broad.
- SFPR can only be distinct if formation is tied to measured recoverability transitions (formation-without-recoverability, recoverability-without-persistence).

Risk:
- very high risk of vague overlap unless formation is defined as a map on target-relevant distinguishability classes.

## III. Structure Persistence

Nearest literature zones:
- robustness/invariance analysis,
- coarse-graining and representation dependence,
- model-mismatch fragility.

Assessment:
- persistence as stability under perturbation is `LITERATURE-KNOWN`.
- SFPR’s potentially distinct piece is the contextwise-exact vs shared-invariant split measured by `CID`.

Risk:
- moderate; needs clean theorem scope to avoid relabeling robustness language.

## IV. Structure Collapse / Indistinguishability

Nearest literature zones:
- identifiability and observability failure,
- function-computation ambiguity,
- indistinguishability and collision no-go families.

Assessment:
- collapse-by-fiber-collision is `LITERATURE-KNOWN` and already core to existing repo mathematics.
- SFPR cannot claim novelty here unless collapse is linked to a new cross-context or design theorem not captured by current spine.

Risk:
- high (already well-covered in both literature and current OCP corpus).

## V. Structure Recoverability

Nearest literature zones:
- observability / quantity-of-interest recovery,
- constrained observation and function recoverability,
- behavioral/data-driven recoverability.

Assessment:
- base recoverability criteria are `LITERATURE-KNOWN` and in-repo known.
- SFPR is plausible only as a scoped synthesis layer: context/invariance/distributed/design interactions under explicit target maps.

Risk:
- high if presented as new core theory; lower if treated as scope-restricted meta-layer with falsification evidence.

## VI. Structure Design (Measurement / Intervention / Augmentation)

Nearest literature zones:
- optimal experimental design (`D`, `A`, `E`, `c`-optimal families),
- sensor placement for quantities of interest,
- intervention design in causal settings,
- multi-terminal/distributed inference.

Assessment:
- target-specific design is `LITERATURE-KNOWN` as a direction.
- SFPR’s plausible distinctness is witness-backed packaging of conflicts: high global information criterion can miss exact target recoverability under fixed budget/context constraints.

Risk:
- moderate; novelty depends on theorem strength and scope discipline.

## Component-Level Verdicts

- `Conditioned-vs-invariant split` (`CID`): `PLAUSIBLY DISTINCT` (supported-family theorem candidate, strong overlap with robust observability but different shared-decoder constraint).
- `Amount-only insufficiency` across rank/count/budget: `LITERATURE-KNOWN` in broad substance; `PLAUSIBLY DISTINCT PACKAGING` in this repo via unified witness catalog.
- `Design criterion conflict` (`D/A/E` vs target alignment): `CLOSE PRIOR ART / REPACKAGED` with practical additive value in this corpus.
- `Intervention lift`: `CLOSE PRIOR ART / REPACKAGED` unless generalized beyond linear context cleaning.
- `Formation-to-recoverability bridge`: currently `OPEN / NOT READY`.

## Safe Positioning Language

Use:
- "branch-limited, falsification-tested synthesis of target-relative structure under context and design constraints"
- "supported-family no-go and split laws"
- "diagnostic invariants with empirical support"

Do not use:
- "new field" or "universal structure theory"
- "replaces observability/information theory"
- "formation law" without mechanism-specific theorem scope.

## Audit Conclusion

SFPR is not literature-free terrain. Most individual ingredients are known. The only defensible additive direction is a narrow, explicitly scoped integration of context-invariant recoverability splits, target-specific design failures, and distributed allocation effects with strict status labeling.
