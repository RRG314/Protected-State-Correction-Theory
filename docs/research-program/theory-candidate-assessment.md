# Theory-Candidate Assessment

## Purpose

This note asks whether the branch results support a stronger theory statement, or only a more careful reframing.

## Candidate Cross-Branch Theory Statements

### Candidate A: One Universal Recoverability / Correction Complexity Theory
Verdict:
- rejected

Why:
- universal scalar-capacity language failed
- cross-domain threshold mechanisms differ materially
- bounded-domain exactness depends on the actual compatible projector, not only on a coarse complexity count

### Candidate B: Protected-variable recovery and protected-state correction are two layers of the same design problem
Verdict:
- survives as a useful organizing statement
- not yet a deep theorem

Why it survives:
- the constrained-observation branch says whether the record can support recovery at all
- the projector/generator branches say what architecture acts once a protected/disturbance split is available
- the design-engine layer turns that into measurement-augmentation and architecture suggestions

What it does not yet do:
- it does not produce one universal theorem across all branches

### Candidate C: Branch-specific capacity, threshold, and no-go invariants are the right mature form of the theory
Verdict:
- strongest surviving theory candidate

Why:
- exact linear branch: rank lower bounds
- restricted-linear observation branch: collision-gap and augmentation deficiency theorems
- sector branch: distinguishability/overlap constraints
- exact bounded-domain branch: compatibility with the actual Hodge decomposition
- asymptotic branch: split-preserving decay and mixing obstructions

This is the strongest honest theory direction because it predicts something nontrivial:
- the right invariant depends on the branch,
- forcing one invariant across branches destroys useful information.

## Current Best Theory Statement

The strongest repo-wide theory statement that survives is:

> Protected-state correction is not governed by one universal scalar law. It is governed by branch-specific exactness, threshold, and no-go invariants tied to the actual decomposition, record, and operator class of the system under study.

This is not flashy, but it is real.

## What Would Upgrade This Further

1. a robust noisy extension of the restricted-linear threshold/augmentation spine
2. a broader bounded-domain exactness theorem beyond the solved finite-mode Hodge family
3. a sharper asymptotic theorem beyond invariant splits

## What To Reject

Reject as theory claims:
- universal phase-transition language
- universal complexity language
- broad “physics everywhere” language
- any statement that sounds deeper than the branch results actually support
