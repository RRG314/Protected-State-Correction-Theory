# Branch Boundary Explanations

Date: 2026-04-17

## 1) Why Branches Are Related

They share a common abstract question:
- when does available structure (`M`, `C`, `S`) preserve/recover target structure (`T`) on admissible family `A`?

They also share common failure motifs:
- collisions/indistinguishability,
- compatibility defects,
- architecture mismatch,
- family and model fragility.

## 2) Why Branches Are Not Identical

### OCP restricted-linear vs soliton restricted nonlinear
- OCP branch has exact row-space/kernel algebra.
- Soliton branch has quotient/symmetry and nonlinear manifold effects.
- Transfer exists only as restricted analogue, not full theorem identity.

### OCP bounded-domain vs MHD closure
- Both involve compatibility obstructions and domain-sensitive exactness.
- MHD closure uses Euler-potential-specific closure equations; OCP bounded-domain lane centers on record/correction compatibility classes.
- Relationship is structural parallel, not equation-level equivalence.

### Theorem branches vs SDS engineering layers
- SDS layers operationalize theorem logic through diagnostics, typed composition, and repair workflows.
- They inherit theorem authority; they do not generate new universal theorem laws by default.

## 3) Boundary Rules (Normative)

1. No branch-to-branch theorem transfer without explicit map translation and hypothesis preservation.
2. If only conceptual similarity survives, status must be `ANALOGY ONLY`.
3. If explicit counterexample exists in target branch, transfer attempt is `REJECTED`.
4. Engineering success alone does not promote theorem status.
