# Final Theory Identity

Date: 2026-04-17
Framework name retained: **Structure-Dependent Recoverability and Failure Theory**.

## 1) Short Master Statement
Exactness and failure are governed by structural compatibility and collision geometry on declared admissible families; beyond this universal core, all stronger laws are branch-limited.

## 2) Medium Statement
The framework unifies in-scope programs by one universal recoverability core and a restricted strengthening layer. The universal core says exact recoverability is factorization/fiber constancy and exact failure occurs under target-distinguishing collisions. Stronger claims (anti-classifier laws, family-enlargement fragility, mismatch instability, boundary/topology obstruction, symmetry-quotient obstruction) are retained only where theorem or validated evidence exists on declared branch classes. Engineering systems (SDS/Discovery Mixer/Structural Discovery) are theorem-linked consequence layers, not theorem-core mathematics.

## 3) Formal Theorem-Style Identity
Let `(X,A,T,M,C,D,~,S)` be a branch instance with admissible family `A`, target `T`, and record map `M`.

### Identity Theorem (scope-limited)
1. **Universal core:**
   - `T` is exactly recoverable from `M` on `A` iff `T` factors through `M` on `A` (equivalently: `T` is constant on fibers of `M`).
   - If `∃ x,x' in A` with `M(x)=M(x')` and `T(x) ≠ T(x')`, exact recoverability on `A` is impossible.
2. **Branch-limited strengthening:**
   - additional laws are promoted only with explicit branch hypotheses and one of: `PROVED`, `PROVED ON SUPPORTED FAMILY`, `CONDITIONAL`, `VALIDATED`, `OPEN`.
3. **Nonclaims:**
   - no universal amount-only classifier,
   - no universal projection-preservation law,
   - no universal emergence/correction equivalence.

Status of identity statement:
- clause (1): `PROVED`.
- clauses (2)-(3): `PROVED` as governance/normalization rules over the promoted theorem stack.

## 4) Plain-Language Explanation
This theory says: what matters most is not how much data or correction you have, but whether the measurement/correction structure matches the target structure on the family you actually care about. If two different states look identical to your records but have different target values, exact recovery is impossible. That is universal. Everything stronger than that is real but scoped: some results are proved only in restricted linear classes, some only on supported nonlinear families, some only as validated engineering behavior.
