# Two Steps Ahead

Date: 2026-04-17

## Step 1: What Is Needed Next to Strengthen the Branch-Limited Theory

### Priority 1 — Continuous upgrade of restricted nonlinear recoverability
- Target: promote soliton same-count and injectivity lanes from finite-family theorems to continuous-class theorems or explicit continuous counterexamples.
- Needed work: quotient-aware analytic injectivity conditions, continuous witness families, not only grid witnesses.

### Priority 2 — Weighted anti-classifier extension
- Target: extend no-budget-only theorem beyond fixed-library unit-cost setting.
- Needed work: weighted sensor libraries, structured-cost proofs/counterexamples.

### Priority 3 — Stability on top of exact factorization
- Target: theorem connecting fiber exactness to continuity/stability margins under perturbations.
- Needed work: modulus-based bounds that survive family perturbation and model mismatch.

### Priority 4 — Broader bounded-domain exactness map
- Target: expand beyond finite-mode Hodge compatible family without fake generalization.
- Needed work: explicit boundary-compatible operator classes and proof/counterexample split.

## Step 2: The Single Most Leveraged Missing Result

### Highest-leverage theorem candidate
A **continuity-aware factorization stability theorem** of the form:

- if `T` factors through `M` on `A` and branch-specific compatibility margin exceeds explicit threshold,
- then perturbations of admissible family/record map produce quantifiable target error bounds,
- and exactness/impossibility transition is controlled by explicit collision-gap growth.

Why this is the biggest move:
- links universal core (`fiber/factorization`) to practical robustness,
- unifies current fragility theorems (`family enlargement`, `model mismatch`) under one quantitative layer,
- would be publishable as a cross-branch strengthening without claiming universal physics.

## Promotion Conditions

Promote a stronger general theory only if:
1. at least one nonlinear branch (currently soliton) reaches continuous theorem-grade injectivity/no-go upgrades,
2. stability-on-factorization theorem survives explicit counterexample pressure,
3. bounded-domain lane gains one additional nontrivial exact class or sharpened impossibility class.

## Kill Conditions

Kill broad unification expansion if:
- continuous upgrades stall and remain finite-family only,
- mismatch/stability statements remain purely branch-local with no transferable quantitative law,
- proposed invariants become decorative and fail to improve classification/prediction.
