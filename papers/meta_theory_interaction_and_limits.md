## Abstract
This note tests a candidate meta-theory motivated by multiple branch results in the OCP ecosystem: exactness and failure may depend on compatibility between interacting structures rather than on single amount-only descriptors. The analysis is falsification-first. We formalize multiple candidate theses, test them against theorem-grade OCP results, restricted soliton recoverability results, Euler-potential MHD closure/obstruction results, and SDS engineering-layer evidence, then separate what survives from what fails. The strongest surviving statement is branch-limited: exact recoverability/correction is controlled by compatibility between target roles and interacting structural roles, while amount-only descriptors are provably insufficient on key supported classes. We reject stronger universal claims, including broad “everything requires two parts” and “one equation is impossible” rhetoric. The outcome is a disciplined exploratory framework, not a universal physical unification claim.

**Keywords:** recoverability, compatibility, anti-classifier, factorization, fiber collisions, model mismatch, branch-limited meta-theory

## 1. Motivation

Several promoted results already show a repeated pattern:
- exactness criteria are structural (fiber/factorization, row-space/kernel compatibility),
- impossibility arises from collisions/indistinguishability,
- amount-only summaries (rank/count/budget) can fail,
- domain/symmetry/model compatibility can flip exactness verdicts.

The motivating question is whether these are isolated artifacts or evidence for a stronger cross-branch principle.

This note does not assume the principle is true. It attempts to formalize and break it.

## 2. Candidate Meta-Theory Statements

Let `S = (A, T, M, C, E, D, ~)` denote admissible family, target, record map, correction architecture, evolution law, disturbance class, and nuisance equivalence relation.

### 2.1 Candidate A (Interaction-requirement)
Nontrivial recoverability/correction requires at least:
1. a target role, and
2. an interacting non-target role,
with exactness controlled by a compatibility condition.

### 2.2 Candidate B (Unification-limit)
No amount-only descriptor can classify exactness across heterogeneous branches; compatibility structure is required.

### 2.3 Candidate C (Representation-distortion)
Exactness fails when representation collapses target-relevant distinctions.

### 2.4 Candidate D (Strong universal multi-role claim)
Every meaningful system requires multiple interacting roles.

Candidate D is included as a deliberate high-risk hypothesis for falsification.

## 3. Role Dictionary

Recurring roles across branches:
- state,
- target,
- record/representation,
- disturbance/ambiguity,
- compatibility condition,
- correction/evolution,
- symmetry/domain constraints (branch-specific).

The recurring mathematical backbone is not “two physical substances,” but “target + interacting structure + compatibility test.”

## 4. Cross-Repo Tests

## 4.1 OCP / recoverability
Tested results include fiber/factorization exactness, collision no-go, no rank-only classifier, no fixed-budget-only classifier, same-rank insufficiency, family-enlargement false positives, model-mismatch instability, and bounded-domain obstruction.

Outcome:
- Candidate A: supported,
- Candidate B: strongly supported on supported classes,
- Candidate C: strongly supported in static recoverability form.

## 4.2 Soliton restricted branch
Tested results include symmetry non-identifiability no-go (`PROVED`), same-count opposite verdict (`VALIDATED + CONDITIONAL`), and projection-preservation/no-go split (`VALIDATED + CONDITIONAL`).

Outcome:
- Candidate A: supported on restricted families,
- Candidate B: restricted support,
- Candidate C: strong support in symmetry-blind observation settings.

## 4.3 MHD closure/obstruction branch
Tested results include constant-resistivity exact classes, variable-resistivity obstruction, annular-vs-axis split, and mixed/tokamak-adjacent restricted branch laws.

Outcome:
- Candidate A: strong branch-limited support,
- Candidate B: support in the sense that profile/domain structure matters beyond scalar amounts,
- Candidate C: partial support (compatibility-loss language stronger than measurement-collapse language).

## 4.4 SDS engineering layer
Structural Discovery and Discovery Mixer provide operational evidence for compatibility-sensitive diagnosis and repair. However, this layer is `VALIDATED` engineering support, not independent theorem generation.

Outcome:
- corroborative evidence only.

## 5. Strongest Surviving Version

### 5.1 Formal surviving statement (branch-limited)
In tested recoverability/correction branches, exactness is controlled by compatibility between target role and interacting structural roles. Failures occur when target-relevant distinctions are not preserved by the adopted representation/operator/domain/symmetry structure.

### 5.2 Status separation
- **Theorem-supported core:** factorization/fiber exactness and collision no-go.
- **Branch-limited theorem support:** anti-classifier package, enlargement fragility, mismatch instability, bounded-domain/domain-compatibility obstructions.
- **Restricted/conditional support:** soliton continuous extensions and projection-preservation generalizations.
- **Engineering support:** SDS tool-layer diagnostics.

## 6. Falsification Outcomes

### 6.1 Weak two-role thesis
Survives on nontrivial recoverability/correction tasks.

### 6.2 Medium compatibility thesis
Survives as branch-limited framework; exact equivalence remains branch-specific.

### 6.3 Strong universal two-role thesis
Fails or becomes tautological.

### 6.4 One-equation rhetoric
Not supported. The strongest defensible claim is narrower: one compressed representation can be insufficient for exactness classification.

## 7. Limits and Nonclaims

This note does **not** claim:
- a theory of everything,
- impossibility of GR/QM unification,
- universal failure of single-equation scientific models.

It claims only that, in the tested branch set, compatibility structure is often indispensable and amount-only compression can be formally insufficient.

## 8. Open Problems

1. Continuous nonlinear anti-classifier theorem in soliton quotient families.
2. Abstract theorem linking static collision no-go and dynamic compatibility no-go without becoming tautological.
3. Transferable quantitative meta-invariants beyond branch-local diagnostics.

## 9. Position Relative to Existing Work

Standard ingredients used here (factorization, identifiability via fibers/kernels, operator compatibility, symmetry-induced non-identifiability) are largely established in adjacent literatures. The likely distinct contribution is packaging and testing them as a falsification-first, branch-limited meta-framework tied to explicit theorem/validated status separation.

## 10. Conclusion

The strongest evidence supports a disciplined middle position:
- reject universal slogans,
- keep a small theorem-backed core,
- promote branch-limited structural compatibility laws,
- retain explicit nonclaims.

This gives a useful organizing framework while staying mathematically honest.
