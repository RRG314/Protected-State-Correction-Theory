# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This repository is meant to support serious technical discussion, careful criticism, and honest collaborative work.

Participants are expected to:

- be respectful and direct
- criticize ideas without attacking people
- be explicit about uncertainty and limitations
- make room for disagreement without turning it into hostility
- help keep the repository useful for contributors, reviewers, and readers from different backgrounds

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable here:

- harassment or personal attacks
- insulting, demeaning, or exclusionary language
- repeated bad-faith argumentation
- knowingly misrepresenting another contributor's work or intent
- pushing speculative claims as settled results after correction
- disruptive behavior that makes collaboration harder for others

## Research-Specific Expectations

Because this is a research repository, the standard also includes:

- do not overclaim
- do not hide counterexamples or negative results
- do not relabel conjectural work as proved work
- do not smuggle analogy-only material into the main supported theory path

## Reporting Problems

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainer at `sreid1118@gmail.com`.

If the problem is specific to a pull request or issue discussion, include a link and a short explanation of what happened.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may remove comments, close threads, or restrict participation when needed to protect the usefulness and integrity of the repository.
