# Next-Phase Paper Lanes

## Lane 1 (Strongest): Quantitative Fragility Of Constrained Recoverability

Working title:
- Alignment, Fragility, and Minimal Repair in Restricted-Linear Recoverability.

Core object:
- recoverability of `Lx` from `Ox` on admissible family `F`.

Core invariant package:
- `r_row`, `theta_def`, `gamma`, `delta`, plus mismatch/noise stress metrics.

What is already strong:
- exactness criterion (`OCP-031`, `OCP-043`),
- same-rank and same-budget anti-classifiers (`OCP-049`, `OCP-050`),
- family-enlargement and mismatch fragility laws (`OCP-052`, `OCP-053`),
- reproducible next-phase quantitative and perturbation artifacts.

Readiness:
- `PARTIAL` (close to paper-ready).

What still needed:
1. weighted-cost anti-classifier theorem,
2. continuity-aware stability theorem,
3. tighter novelty comparison versus classical identifiability/conditioning literature.

## Lane 2: Bounded-Domain Compatibility And Obstruction

Working title:
- Boundary-Compatible Exactness vs Obstruction in Bounded Projection Recovery.

Core object:
- bounded-domain exact correction under boundary conditions and topology constraints.

Core invariant candidates:
- boundary compatibility index,
- harmonic/topological obstruction terms,
- boundary-normal residual ratios.

What is already strong:
- transplant no-go (`OCP-023`),
- divergence-only bounded no-go (`OCP-028`),
- finite-mode compatible exact theorem (`OCP-044`),
- next-phase quantitative boundary-ratio witnesses.

Readiness:
- `PARTIAL` (branch-limited but serious).

What still needed:
1. sharper theorem-level compatibility index,
2. broader explicit bounded families,
3. stronger topology-sensitive lower bounds.

## Lane 3 (Secondary): Dynamic Thresholds And Asymptotic Recovery

Working title:
- Finite-Time Impossibility and Asymptotic Threshold Laws for Dynamic Correction.

Status:
- `OPEN/PARTIAL`.

Why secondary:
- finite-time no-go and asymptotic spectral laws are strong,
- finite-history threshold program is still example-driven and not yet theorem-complete across families.

## Recommended Priority

1. Lane 1 first (best theorem/no-go/validation balance).
2. Lane 2 second (strong domain application with clear obstruction and practical relevance).
3. Lane 3 as targeted extension, not main near-term paper lane.
